Conditionals are constructed from two clauses:

An "if" clause (antecedent).

and

A "consequence clause."

“If” may sometimes be replaced with “as long as,” “unless,” “provided that,” “should” or “were.”

It does not matter which clause comes first. However, if the "if" clause comes first, the clauses must be separated by a comma.

Zero Conditional

"If you enter the water, you get wet” - indisputable fact - present simple (regular action).

First Conditional

“If I earn some money, I will spend it” - possible, probable, likely event (future).

Second Conditional

"If I won the lottery, I would travel around the world" - very unlikely, wishful thinking (future).

"If I had wings, I could fly" - hypothetical, impossible (present).

Third Conditional

“If I had earned some money, I would have spent it” - imaginary situation in the past.

Mixed Conditional

"If I had studied harder, I would have a good job now” - the "if" clause is in the past and the consequence is in the present.
(There are several types of mixed conditional, this is the simplest and enough for up to level 7.5).
